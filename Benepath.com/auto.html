What Affects Your Rate

Location

Your ZIP code affects rates based on local accident rates, weather, and theft statistics. Urban areas typically cost more than rural.

Vehicle Type

Make, model, year, and safety ratings all factor in. Expensive or high-performance vehicles cost more to insure.

Driving Record

Accidents, violations, and DUIs significantly raise premiums. A clean record earns you major discounts across carriers.

Age and Experience

Teen and senior drivers typically face higher rates. Rates often stabilize and improve in your mid-20s through 50s.

Credit Score

In most states, insurers use a credit-based score. Better credit generally translates to lower premiums — sometimes significantly.

Annual Mileage

The more you drive, the more exposure you have to accidents. Low-mileage drivers often qualify for usage-based discounts.

FAQ

What are the types of car insurance?

Liability insurance is mandatory in most states, with some states also requiring personal injury protection and uninsured motorist coverage. You can also buy collision and comprehensive coverage, which both insure your car against physical damages, including striking another car, fire, theft, hail and more.

What is the cheapest car insurance?

If you want the cheapest insurance possible, you’ll want to adjust your policy so that it meets only your state’s minimum insurance requirements. However, this will decrease how much coverage you have in a car accident and you’ll have to pay out of pocket for your own vehicle’s repair costs.

How to get car insurance?

The best way to buy car insurance is to compare rates. Not only do car insurance rates change daily, but you will get different pricing from each insurer.

What Affects Your Rate

Number of Employees

The size of your workforce directly impacts your group rate. Larger groups often qualify for better pricing, while smaller businesses may have fewer plan options available.

Employee Age & Health Demographics

Insurers look at the overall age and health profile of your employee pool. A younger, healthier workforce typically results in lower premiums across the group.

Coverage Level & Plan Type

The benefits you choose for your employees — deductibles, copays, and network type — all affect your rate. PPO plans generally cost more than HMOs due to broader provider access.

Industry & Occupation

Some industries carry higher health risk than others. Physical or high-hazard occupations may result in higher premiums compared to desk-based workforces.

Location

Healthcare costs vary significantly by state and region. Your business's ZIP code affects both the cost of plans available and the provider networks accessible to your employees.

Employer Contribution

The percentage of the premium your business covers versus what employees pay affects your overall cost. Higher employer contributions increase your spend but improve employee uptake and retention.

FAQ

Do I have to offer health insurance to my employees?

Businesses with 50 or more full-time employees are required by law to offer health coverage under the Affordable Care Act. Smaller businesses aren't mandated to, but offering coverage can be a major advantage for attracting and retaining talent.

How much of the premium am I required to cover as an employer?

There's no federal minimum, but most carriers require employers to cover at least 50% of the employee's premium to qualify for a group plan. Your contribution level can also affect how many employees opt in.

Can part-time employees be included in a group plan?

It depends on the carrier and the plan. Some group plans allow part-time employees to be covered, but eligibility rules vary. We'll surface plans that match your specific workforce structure.

What's the difference between a group plan and individual health insurance?

Group plans are purchased by the employer and cover all eligible employees under one policy, usually at a lower per-person cost than individual plans. Employees typically get broader coverage for less out-of-pocket than they'd find shopping on their own.
